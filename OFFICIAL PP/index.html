Development

Across All Grades

Each phase has a different football need, so the coaching focus changes as players mature.

Foundation Phase

Fun, engaging sessions that develop coordination, confidence and a love for the game. Players learn fundamental movement patterns, basic ball control and the importance of teamwork.

Intermediate Phase

Players begin building a stronger technical foundation through structured training and game-based learning. Sessions focus on ball mastery, passing, receiving and making better decisions under pressure.

Senior Primary

Training introduces greater tactical awareness and positional understanding while continuing to refine technical ability. Players are encouraged to think independently, solve problems and apply skills in realistic match situations.

Junior High School

Sessions become more competitive and demanding, with a stronger focus on tactical discipline, athletic development and consistency. Players learn to take greater responsibility for their preparation, performance and growth.

Senior High School

Training is designed to prepare players for high-level school football through advanced tactical concepts and performance standards. Leadership, resilience and game management become increasingly important parts of development.

First Teams

High-performance coaching focused on maximising team success and individual potential. Preparation centres around match execution, accountability, competitive excellence and maintaining standards on and off the pitch.

Director

Khumo Modise

Director | Pitch Partners

Khumo Modise is a Jeppe High School Old Boy who was awarded Football Full Colours and developed through the Wits Juniors and Sporting Lisbon development systems, having the opportunity to compete in international tournaments throughout his youth. During his time at Jeppe, he was part of the team that won the prestigious St David's Challenge Cup in 2022 and finished as the tournament's leading goal scorer.

He later earned a football scholarship to the United States, where he played for Tennessee Wesleyan University and DMV Elite FC, a semi professional club based in the Washington and Maryland area, while also coaching youth players aged 6 to 13 at McMinn County.

After returning to South Africa, Khumo coached the Jeppe High School Second Team to a near perfect season before continuing his coaching journey at Reddam Waterfall. Through Pitch Partners, he draws on his experiences as both a player and coach to help schools create environments where young athletes can develop, compete and reach their full potential.

"Talent becomes meaningful only when structure gives it direction."

Information & Terms

Clear Standards. Professional Delivery.

These website terms give schools, parents and players a clear overview of how Pitch Partners operates. Formal school partnership agreements are handled separately where required.

Terms & Conditions

For Schools

Schools engage Pitch Partners (Pty) Ltd on a monthly retainer basis. Each partnership is tailored to the school's football programme and may include coaching services, match-day support, tournament assistance, programme planning and ongoing development support.

Retainer fees are agreed before services commence and are invoiced monthly. Payment is due within 7 days of invoice unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Where equipment, programme setup, operational planning or additional resources are required, any onboarding requirements and fees are discussed through consultation and agreed before commencement.

For Private Training Clients

Private coaching sessions must be booked at least 24 hours in advance and are subject to coach availability. Payment is required before the session begins to confirm the booking.

Sessions cancelled more than 24 hours before the scheduled start time may be rescheduled at no additional cost. Sessions cancelled within 24 hours, or missed without notice, may be charged in full.

Player Pathways

Players must first be assessed through PP before pathway guidance or introductions are considered. Trials, scholarships, contracts, selections and placements are never guaranteed.

Media & Photography

Photos and videos may occasionally be captured during coaching activities for promotional or educational purposes. Where PP wishes to use player images or video, players, parents and schools may be asked to sign a POPIA/media consent waiver. Consent is voluntary, and anyone who is not comfortable may decline without affecting their involvement in PP programmes.

Privacy Policy

Personal Information

PP may collect names, phone numbers, email addresses, school details and enquiry information submitted through the website consultation form or direct communication.

How Information Is Used

This information is used to respond to enquiries, arrange consultations, manage coaching services and communicate with schools, parents, players and coaches.

Data Protection

PP treats personal information with care in line with the principles of South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA). PP does not sell personal information to third parties. Information is only shared where necessary to deliver services, communicate with relevant parties or comply with applicable law.
